Overview

BU CS Ambassadors (BUCSA) hosted a web design competition to redesign their entire website.

Over the course of two weeks, my team and I came together to create the new face for BUCSA. As a student group that aimed to build connections through mentorship, our goal was not only to add color to a website, but to develop layers of depth in the meaning of their brand. Although I was the only web developer on the team, AKA Designs was successful in earning first place for our project.

My Contribution

The timing of the project fell right after finals week for Boston University students. My teammates, Alice and Katie, were occupied with vacation plans and internship transitions, and so I took the lead product designer role in this endeavor. Furthermore, I developed the entire website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, implementing a Node.js back-end on an Express.js framework to add functionality to a mailing list and contact form.

Our Solution

Our solution was both a full website redesign that emphasized the focus on the mentor-mentee program. It utilized modern design trends and pulled from the Boston University design system with the reds and blacks to visually appeal to incoming computer science students. Find the demo video further below and the GitHub repo here.

Kickoff

Problem

BUCSA had a WordPress website that simply was not attracting incoming students.

The initiative behind the competition, according to the kickoff presentation, was that BUCSA was in the process of getting their own official BU domain and saw this as an opportunity to rebuild the website from scratch for more control and flexibility.


Unfortunately, both as someone who isn't affiliated with BUCSA and as a UX designer doing their research, there was not too much information to work with.

Challenges

With a lack of content material, design system, and time to work with, I put together a goal-oriented approach that would let me research, design, and implement within the two-week timeframe. To meet the BU website requirement, I drew inspiration from Boston University, BU Department of Computer Science, and student groups including FYSOP, BU AKPsi, and BU AXS (I designed this website too, but it uses Squarespace).

Research

Audience

BUCSA is a student organization whose mission is to serve as a platform for a more collaborative, inclusive, and unified computer science community.


We were able to hold a few chats and conversations with 4 students ranging from BUCSA members to BU students. Focusing primarily on the interests of our client, we wanted to better understand the pain points of the current system and in broader scope, what the mission of BUCSA is to form user personas.

Questions

"How might we increase the retention rate/decrease the bounce rate of users from the landing screen?"

"How might we draw more attention to the mentorship program?"

"How might we limit the number of clicks to traverse across the website?"

"How might we emphasize the importance of joining student groups?"

Goal


How do we create an experience for BU CS students to drive a need for a community through the Ambassadors platform?

Personas

A BU student interested in joining BUCSA

A BU CS Ambassador

Key Takeaways

While we knew it was critical to focus on the students who would be interested in getting involved as an Ambassador or the mentorship program, we also discovered that other Ambassadors would be using the website during our research.


Although a login system with content and features limited only to members was a listed desirable by the client, we recognized that the limited scope of the two week timeframe made it infeasible to integrate. As a result, our efforts were allocated to emphasize a resources page for both current and prospective members.
